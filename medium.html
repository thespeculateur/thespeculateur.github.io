Reasons Why One Becomes A Currency Trader

First of all, currency or forex trading is a risky endeavor and we do not convince anybody to be one.

However, we conducted an informal survey in Babypips website, one of the most reputable resources of currency trading and it shows that independence, flexibility and challenge are the major reasons why one chooses to become a currency trader. Thanks to the proliferation of web technologies, forex is available almost to everybody and not just exclusively for financial institutions.

Here's the link of the survey and the answers provided were very interesting, funny and very informative: https://forums.babypips.com/t/im-curious-guys-what-got-you-into-forex-trading/175198.

Forex Heatmap As An Exit Tool

On Feb 7, 2019 at 5:00 pm, we entered a short position in GBP/USD pair. Around after two hours, the heatmap went like this and we tweeted it.

What a chaos! Careful guys, careful! pic.twitter.com/Z2Csp3IgYx

— The Speculateur (@TheSpeculateur_) February 7, 2019

Green box means that the corresponding pair went up while the red box means it went down. By shorting GBP/USD, we are technically "long" USD. However after two hours, the direction of USD is in chaos as shown in the yellow rounded corner box. Being a prudent trader, without a doubt we exited that trade and took a small profit rather than risk losing that trade. It could have profited more, but the risk management principle we employed will yield more gains in the long run.
